Health & Safety

Simple guidance for safer paint storage, handling, and use.

Good paint results also depend on good site habits. Use these basic Wall King precautions during storage, preparation, application, and cleanup.

Before application

Prepare the space and the surface carefully.

Keep the work area ventilated, protect surrounding surfaces, and make sure walls are dry, stable, and clean before primer or finish coats are applied.

  • Open windows or provide airflow during painting.
  • Keep children and pets away from freshly painted spaces.
  • Use clean tools and follow the recommended dilution and drying guidance.

Handling and storage

Store containers securely and avoid unnecessary exposure.

Keep paint containers tightly closed, upright, and away from direct sun, sparks, and extreme heat. Avoid contact with eyes and prolonged skin exposure during use.

  • Store in a cool, dry place away from food items.
  • Wear basic protective gear such as gloves during application and cleanup.
  • Do not reuse empty containers for household storage.
Step 1

Ventilate the room

Fresh airflow helps reduce discomfort while painting and during the early drying period.

Step 2

Handle with care

Avoid splashes, seal containers after use, and clean tools responsibly after each session.

Step 3

Dispose responsibly

Do not pour leftover paint into drains. Follow local disposal practices for coatings and containers.
